Plant Information


Plant Name: Kela


Benefits: The banana plant, including its fruit, leaves, flowers, and stem, offers numerous health and medicinal benefits. Bananas are rich in essential nutrients, including potassium, vitamin C, and vitamin B6, which support heart health, aid digestion, and boost immunity. Banana leaves are used in various culinary traditions for their antibacterial properties and their ability to enhance flavors when food is wrapped and steamed within them. The plant's sap and flower are known to have healing properties, helping to reduce inflammation and regulate blood sugar. Overall, the Kela plant plays a significant role in both nutrition and holistic medicine.



Season: Banana plants thrive in warm and humid climates, making them perennial plants that grow year-round in tropical and subtropical regions. While they don’t have a specific season, bananas generally take 9 to 12 months from planting to produce fruit, and individual stalks produce fruit only once before being cut down. After the initial harvest, new shoots, or "suckers," continue to grow, maintaining year-round productivity. The absence of a strict seasonality makes bananas a staple, with availability and harvesting continuing almost continuously throughout the year.



Region: The banana plant is native to Southeast Asia but has spread to nearly all tropical and subtropical regions globally. Major banana-producing regions include South Asia (India, the Philippines), Latin America, Central and South America, and Africa. India is one of the largest producers of bananas in the world, with states like Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, and Andhra Pradesh specializing in its cultivation. The plant thrives in regions with well-drained, rich soil, abundant sunlight, and a warm climate, typically ranging between 25°C to 30°C.



Age: The banana plant is generally a fast-growing perennial that can reach maturity and produce fruit within 9 to 12 months, depending on the conditions. Each banana stalk, or pseudostem, lives for around a year, bearing fruit once before it is cut down to allow new stalks to grow from the rhizome (underground stem). Although individual stalks are short-lived, the banana plant itself can live and continuously produce new stalks for decades under favorable conditions, effectively making it a long-lasting perennial through its regenerating suckers.
